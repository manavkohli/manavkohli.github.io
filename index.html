Intro

I grew up in Portland, OR and am currently studying Computer Science at Claremont McKenna College. I love to build things, and CS seemed like the most obvious way to learn, challenge myself, and practically apply something that can be abstract. In true liberal-arts-student-form, I hope to use programming as one of many tools to contribute to a product team alongside my experience in growth, finance, and fintech.

I have a lot of ideas, and am willing to bet 5% of them are good. Some of the better include a basic community music listening web app, some research I pursued on binary star mass transfer, and 'Refridge-raiders', a refridgerator cleaning service.

Aside from school and terrible ideas, I've been fortunate enough to travel extensively. In the last three years I've visited 4 continents and 9 countries, meanwhile working on a farm exchange in Brazil, traveling and interning in Mongolia, and backpacking through central Europe.

POLITICAL RESEARCH

California Prison Realignment, 2014

Wrote and published a paper analyzing the effect of California Prison Realignment policy on counties. The article provided an in-depth analysis of key legislation, the weight placed on county jails, and the and shift in sentencing to accomodate the movement of prisoners into the County systems. The research was published in the Rose Institute's 2014 Inland Empire Outlook.

Demographic Analysis of Riverside and San Bernardino Counties, 2013

Wrote and published an analysis of the social, economic, and demographic shifts within the Inland Empire. Used ArcGIS software to create visual representations of the changes within the area.

Redistricting in America, 2013

I completed comprehensive reports on changes to Congressional Districts in New York, Oklahoma, Hawaii, and Oregon following the 2010 census. For each district within the states I analyzed demographic, political, and economic shifts as well as any geographical changes to their borders. The research is held within a Redistricting in America, a site maintained by the Rose Institute of State and Local Government that holds information on other states as well as relevant legislation.

SCIENCE RESEARCH

Astrometric Measurements of V1191 CYGNUS, 2012

Observed V1191 CYGNUS' orbital period and assessed the unusual mass transfer rate between it and its secondary star. Our analysis suggested that the abnormal rates were caused by a tertiaty star, and our research lead us to become semi-finalists in the Intel Science and Engineering Fair.
