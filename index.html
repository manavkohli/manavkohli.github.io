SCIENCE RESEARCH

Differential Photometry of V1191 CYGNUS, 2012

Observed V1191 CYGNUS' orbital period and assessed the unusual mass transfer rate between it and its secondary star. In comparing our data to previous research, we found that the increasing period was not accounted for. Considering the low mass ratio but positive rate of change of period and high rate of mass transfer, we hypothesized that mass transfer is not a one way, but rather a two-way transfer between the primary and secondary star.

Astrometric Measurement of the Visual Double Star Delta Bootis, 2010

Observed Delta Bootis and used Astrometric measurements to verify past research suggesting that it was a visual double star. The research was conducted with the Oregon Episcopal School at the 2010 Astronomy Research Seminar at the Pine Mountain Observatory in Central Oregon, and ultimately published in the Journal of Double Star Observation.
